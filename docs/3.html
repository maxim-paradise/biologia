METAMORPHOSIS

01 Ovum

THE EGG

Stage 01 — Gestation

A female butterfly lays her eggs on a leaf. These eggs are tiny, varying in shape from spherical to cylindrical. The choice of leaf is critical — it must provide immediate sustenance for the hatching larva.


OBSERVATION: The ribbed texture functions as a structural defense against predators and environmental stress.

⚡ DURATION
🔬 MICRO-DETAIL
🌿 HOST PLANT
Eggs hatch in just 3–7 days depending on temperature and species.
Egg surfaces are covered in microscopic ridges called chorion, allowing gas exchange for the developing embryo.
Females chemically analyse leaves with their feet before laying, ensuring correct nutrition for larvae.
REF: OVM-001.X
02 Larva

CATERPILLAR

Stage 02 — Consumption

The primary function of the larva is to eat. Upon hatching, it consumes its own eggshell before attacking the host plant. It grows exponentially, shedding its exoskeleton in a process called molting.


OBSERVATION: Vibrant banding is aposematic coloration — a visual warning to predators indicating toxicity.

⚡ GROWTH RATE
🦟 PREDATORS
🧬 MOLTING
A caterpillar increases its body mass by up to 100× in just a few weeks.
Over 90% of butterfly eggs and larvae are eaten before reaching the pupal stage.
Molting occurs 4–5 times. Each phase is called an "instar" — the larva is briefly vulnerable with a soft new skin.
REF: LRV-002.Y
03 Pupa

CHRYSALIS

Stage 03 — Transformation

Within the hardened chrysalis, radical biological breakdown occurs. The caterpillar digests itself into a nutrient soup. "Imaginal discs" — dormant stem cells — then build the adult butterfly's entire anatomy.


OBSERVATION: What appears still is violently active. This is not sleep — it is total cellular obliteration and reconstruction.

⚡ DURATION
🔬 INSIDE
🛡 DEFENCE
The pupal stage lasts 10–14 days in summer, but some species overwinter in chrysalis for months.
The body of a caterpillar literally dissolves into a liquid — only 50 or so imaginal disc cells survive to build the adult.
Some chrysalises vibrate and emit clicking sounds to repel predators. Others mimic leaves, bark, or bird droppings.
REF: PPA-003.Z
04 Imago

BUTTERFLY

Stage 04 — Reproduction

The adult emerges with crumpled wings pumped full of hemolymph to expand and harden. Its sole biological purpose is reproduction — continuing the eternal cycle.


OBSERVATION: Wing patterns are formed by thousands of microscopic overlapping scales that absorb and reflect light through structural interference.

⚡ LIFESPAN
🌍 MIGRATION
👁 VISION
Most adult butterflies live only 2–4 weeks. The exception: monarchs live up to 8 months to complete their migration.
Monarch butterflies migrate up to 4,500 km — navigating using a time-compensated sun compass.
Butterflies can see ultraviolet light invisible to humans, revealing hidden patterns on wings used for mate selection.
REF: IMG-004.W
